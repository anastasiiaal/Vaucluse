Vaucluse : We are all in need of colors

Places not to miss

Palais des Papes

In Avignon there are several sites to see, among them - the magnificent 14th century Gothic palace, former fortress and palace of the popes, one of the most visited French monuments.

On map

Le Mont Ventoux

Absolutely unmissable site when visiting Vaucluse, Mont Ventoux is a majestic summit which rises to an altitude of 1,910 meters.

On map

Pont Saint-Bénézet

The famous Avignon bridge known thanks to the song. Listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it was partly destroyed, and of the 22 original arches, only 4 remain.

On map

Notre-Dame-des Doms

Historical monument dating from the 12th, 14th and 17th centuries, it is next to the Palais des Papes. You can contemplate the beautiful facade and its bell tower, surmounted by a golden virgin.

On map

Le Colorado Provençal

An outdoor activity to do in the Vaucluse. The landscapes and colors of the site will not leave you indifferent. This Historic Monument is an old ocher quarry.

On map

Les champs de lavande

Lavender fields are emblematic of Provence. Usually from mid-June to early August (may vary depending on the weather) lavender is in bloom.

On map

L’abbaye de Sénanque

Sénanque Abbey, founded in 1148, is located in the town of Gordes. It is still occupied by monks who cultivate lavandin and olive trees on their land.

On map

Dentelles de Montmirail

Outdoor enthusiasts can embark on an adventure in the Dentelles de Montmirail massif, which offers a magnificent panorama, as well as beautiful steep walls.

On map

Vignobles du Vaucluse

Viticulture is very important in the Vaucluse, being one of the main agricultural activities. It is possible to make a route with the areas open to visitors.
